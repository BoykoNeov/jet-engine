Rungs 7–10, 19 · the combustor story

The NO bell, and the quench that re-makes it

Thermal NO lives in a narrow, hot window near stoichiometric. Resolve the primary zone (rung 8) and EI_NO jumps six orders into the ICAO band; run the primary rich (rung 9) and it collapses again — that is why RQL combustors burn rich. But the quench back to lean must cross the peak of the bell…

EI_NO vs primary equivalence ratio — the bell
Log scale: the lean flank climbs with flame temperature, the rich flank collapses as the O-starved pool crashes [O]. Rung 19 adds two honest lifts: super-equilibrium O (T-driven — weakest exactly where the pool is rich) and De Soete prompt NO (which survives where thermal dies).

The quench path: T(β) through dilution
A rich primary (φp=1.4) heats up on its way to lean — it must cross stoichiometric, the bell's peak. A lean primary just cools. The moving dot is the mixing pocket.
What a slow quench costs — EI_NO vs quench time τq
The ideal (infinitely fast) quench freezes the rich primary's tiny NO. Every extra millisecond spent near the stoich crossing re-makes NO — "low-NOx only if the quench is fast."

Rungs 11–13, 22–23 · the mixing optimum

Mean-field mixing has no optimum. Variance does.

Derive the quench time from the dilution jets (rung 11) and EI_NO falls monotonically with jet momentum — stronger jet, faster quench, less NO, no optimum. Only when the model carries spatial variance (rungs 12–13) does the curve turn back up, and the minimum pins itself at the Holdeman group Copt≈2.5 that combustor designers actually use.

EI_NO vs jet momentum-flux ratio J — three closures
Mean-field bulk (rung 11) falls forever. The two-stream unmixedness model (rung 12) turns back up. The resolved β-PDF (rung 13) puts a sharp notch exactly at Jopt=16, where C=(S/H)√J = Copt.
Rung 22 — the optimum becomes an output
Resolve the dilution cross-plane and compute the segregation g for three different combustor geometries. Plotted against J they disagree; replot against the Holdeman group C=(S/H)√J and they collapse onto one curve with its minimum at C≈2.5 — no Copt was fed in anywhere.
Rung 23 — the rich pockets dwell longest, and that adds NO
Rung 22 derived the cross-plane's width but still quenched every pocket for the same scalar time. Develop that cross-plane in time and each pocket gets its own dwell τ(ξ): the dilution jet reaches the rich pockets last, so they linger longest. Plotted is the correlated model divided by a matched-mean twin — same variance, same mean dwell ⟨τ⟩, correlation removed — which isolates the correlation and nothing else. It sits above 1 everywhere: rich-dwells-long makes NO that one shared τ structurally cannot express. The effect is largest under-penetration (≈+5.6%), decays through Copt, and then flattens onto a residual ≈+1% plateau rather than dying — the correlation stops shrinking, it never goes away. Every pocket here stays formation-limited (max a<1), which is why the sign never flips. Read the plateau's shape, not its ripple: that wiggle is finer than the quadrature's own ~0.5% resolution.
What rung 23 does not claim: the correlation's shape is derived — its sign, and its concentration under-penetration. The magnitude rides on rung 11's un-anchored mixing time τ_mix, exactly as rung 22's Copt≈2.5 rides on k_p. And the emissions global minimum is still not claimed: the derived ⟨τ⟩ falls off-optimum where rung 16's imposed τ_core grows — opposite-trending, both un-anchored. Rung 16 already declined that location; rung 23 does not take it back.

Rungs 14, 17, 19–21 · three inversions

Where the intuition fails

The last rungs are a study in honest surprises: the "rich primary explodes" intuition fails from both directions, and the mixed-out shortcut every simple model takes turns out to hide super-equilibrium NO in the exhaust.

Super-equilibrium O is T-driven, not rich-driven
The Westenberg multiplier m(T) on equilibrium [O] shrinks as the gas gets hotter — so it is smallest exactly where NO is actually made (the ~2450 K stoich crossing), and the effective lift is modest (~×1.15–1.28).
Prompt NO survives where thermal dies
Ratio of prompt to thermal EI across the primary sweep — it grows monotonically rich (thermal collapses with [O]; prompt rides De Soete's fitted φ-bump with a single, ~27× milder Arrhenius exponential).
The exhaust-NO clamp ladder (rung 17)
Carry three mixing-fidelity models of the same rich (φp=1.5) combustor through the same nozzle cooling to T9, and read a = [NO]/[NO]eq(T9). The mixed-out shortcut reads dormant (a≪1); the bulk quench and per-pocket models fire — the exhaust really is super-equilibrium, and averaging the combustor away hides it. Rung 20's super-eq O lifts every numerator; the thermodynamic denominator never moves.
The negative result that earns its keep (rung 18): a 0-D variance transport with any mean-field mixing rate ω(J) gives a monotone g(J) — no interior optimum. The Holdeman optimum appears only once the jet spacing S enters the rate. Rung 22 then inverts this honestly: resolve the cross-plane, and Copt emerges as an output.
