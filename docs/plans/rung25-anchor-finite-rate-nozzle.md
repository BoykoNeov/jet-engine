## The headline the numbers forced — a THREE-state picture, not a two-bound interpolation

The plan (and an early advisor read) assumed the finite-rate flow interpolates between rung-14's
**two** bounds: `Da→0 =` frozen, `Da→∞ =` reversible equilibrium. **The numbers refuted the second
half.** The finite-rate expansion starts from the **frozen-in station-4 mixture**, which arrives at
the nozzle **super-equilibrium** (it was equilibrated at the hotter `Tt4` and frozen through the
turbine to `Tt9 < Tt4`, so it carries more dissociation than local equilibrium). A **real
(irreversible) flow must re-equilibrate that entry**, and that re-equilibration is irreversible — an
entropy source that **survives even at infinite rate**. So there are **three** reference states:

| | state | how computed | reduces? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(F)** | **frozen** (`Da→0`) | rung-14 frozen `_expand_nozzle(shifting=False)` | **YES — exact, the reduce contract** |
| **(I)** | **irreversible-fast** (`Da→∞`) — the new **ATTAINABLE ceiling** | closed form: equilibrate the frozen entry at constant `(H, pt9)` → `(comp*, T*)`, then rung-14 reversible shifting from there to `p9` | it is the finite-rate `Da→∞` limit |
| **(R)** | **reversible-shift** | rung-14 `_expand_nozzle(shifting=True)` — a **strict, UNREACHABLE ceiling** | **NO — deliberately. The non-reduction IS the finding.** |

The rung **reduces to rung-14 FROZEN exactly and deliberately does NOT reduce to rung-14
equilibrium.** The gap **(R−I)** is the "sliver of entry irreversibility" the rung-14 spec named and
set aside (§ "the load-bearing modeling call"); rung 25 **quantifies** it. The finite-rate flow lives
in `[F, I]`; the reversible bound `R` sits a small but real distance **above** the attainable ceiling.

- **Both gaps vanish at `Tt4=1500`** (entry dissociation ≈ 5×10⁻⁶ ⇒ no entry non-equilibrium ⇒ F,
  I, R collapse). Gap **(R−I) = 0.001%** — the entry irreversibility is a genuine sliver here.
- **Both gaps grow monotonically with `Tt4`.** At `Tt4=2200` the **attainable** gap (I−F) is
  **0.43%** of `V9`, and the **unreachable** gap (R−I) is **0.031%** — so a fast real nozzle
  recovers ~**93%** of rung-14's equilibrium bracket, and ~**7%** is forever lost to entry
  re-equilibration irreversibility. This is exactly rung 14's "dormant lean, earns its keep hot."
- **Robustness split (do not certify the number):** the **existence and sign** of the (R−I) gap are
  **thermodynamically robust** — the availability loss of relaxing an out-of-equilibrium entry is a
  pure thermodynamic quantity, independent of the relaxation law. The **magnitude** (0.031%) and the
  interior curve shape ride on the model (the relaxation closure + the frozen-entry choice).

## (2) THE KEYSTONE — the integrator's `Da→∞` asymptote == the closed-form (I)

The closed-form irreversible-fast ceiling (I) is a **rate-law-independent** reference. The marching
integrator, pushed to large `Da` (fully pinning the composition to local equilibrium), converges to it:

```
  Tt4=2200:  closed-form (I) = 1412.5755
  integrator Da=300  nstep=1200: V9=1412.4919   (diff −0.0836)
  integrator Da=1000 nstep=4000: V9=1412.5034   (diff −0.0722)     ← residual is truncation, → 0
  (Tt4=2000: diff −0.0161 ;  Tt4=1500: diff −0.0002)
```

This match is the **certification** that the ~7%-short asymptote is the **physically correct**
`Da→∞` limit, not a numerical failure. (The early prototypes' "failure to reach 1413.01" WAS the
model being right.) The integrator is **not** dispatched to `R` at `Da=∞` — that would paste a
discontinuity onto its own curve; `I` is the true endpoint.

- **`V9(Da)` climbs monotonically** from frozen toward the attainable ceiling `I` (attain-filled =

- **`dS ≥ 0` everywhere** (2nd law), **peaking at intermediate `Da`** (≈0.067 near `Da=1–3`) where

  → 0 as `Da→0` (frozen, no reaction) and → the entry-jump floor (~0.015) as `Da→∞`.
- **CO burns out** monotonically with `Da` (8.2×10⁻³ → 1.0×10⁻⁶): the recombination the velocity
  gain rides on, made visible in composition.

`Da` is a **cartoon knob** (a normalized-schedule Damköhler, NOT an Arrhenius-anchored time — see the
spec's freezing concession). The **interior curve rides on it**; the **three-state picture and the
(R−I) gap do not**.

## (4) The FROZEN reduce — `Da→0` → (F), convergent (2nd-order in 1/nstep)

The marching integrator at tiny `Da` converges to the exact frozen bound `V9_F=1406.5394`:

```
  nstep= 100: V9(Da=1e-4)=1406.56935   |V9−V9_F|=2.99e-02
  nstep= 400: V9(Da=1e-4)=1406.54161   |V9−V9_F|=2.16e-03
  nstep=1600: V9(Da=1e-4)=1406.53987   |V9−V9_F|=4.29e-04
```

This is a **CONVERGENT reduce with a stated tolerance**, NOT bit-for-bit (the ODE integrator carries
truncation) — the same honest departure rung 14 flagged as "convergent, not identity." **The exact
frozen bound is the DISPATCHED rung-14 value** (`Da=0` returns `_expand_nozzle(shifting=False)`); the
integrator only runs for interior `Da`, and this gate certifies it lands on the bound in the limit.

## The numerical scheme the shipped code uses (settled by the prototype race)

The interior integrator marches along a **geometric pressure schedule** `p(s) = pt9·(p9/pt9)^s`,
`s ∈ [0,1]` (the progress coordinate — **linear in `ln p`**; declared, sets interpolation shape only):

- **Composition** — exact linear relaxation over the step, `Δn = (1 − e^{−Da·ds})(n_eq(T,p) − n)`.
  Unconditionally stable for the stiff relaxation (a raw explicit `Da·(n_eq−n)` step / RK4 **blows
  up** at large `Da` — tested, rejected). Evaluated at the step-start target (interior `Da` is
  scheme-independent to <0.01 m/s vs corrected/implicit variants — tested).
- **Energy / temperature** — the exact `dh = v·dp` relation (energy + momentum, holds for ANY
  adiabatic frictionless flow, reversible or not), integrated **implicitly** per step by bisecting
  `T1` on `H_abs(comp1,T1) − H_abs(comp0,T0) = ½(v0 + v1)·dp`, `v = n_tot·Ru·T/p` (per mol air).
  2nd-order; the frozen limit converges as (4).
- The `Da→∞` closed form (I) and both rung-14 bounds (F, R) are computed directly (no marching).

**Stability.** The shipped scheme is **unconditionally stable in `Da`**: the composition step is the
exact linear-relaxation fraction `relax = 1 − e^{−Da·ds} ∈ [0,1]` for any `Da`, and the trapezoid
energy is solved implicitly — so even `Da = 10⁸` at `nstep = 400` is well-behaved (`dS = +0.017`,
`V9` below the reversible ceiling). The **only** error is the trapezoid truncation, 2nd-order in
`1/nstep`; below `nstep ≈ 50` it overshoots (the exit creeps past the reversible ceiling, `dS < 0`),
so `FiniteRate` requires `nstep ≥ 100` and `_finite_rate_expand` asserts `dS ≥ −5e-3` as the
coarse-grid net. `Da = ∞` is never marched — it dispatches to the closed-form (I).

**Rejected alternatives (recorded so they are not re-tried):** forward-Euler on `dh=v·dp` is only
1st-order (needs ~25 600 steps for the frozen bound); RK4 is stiff-unstable at large `Da`; operator
splitting (isentropic-frozen ⊕ const-p react) mis-allocates recombination energy between reheat and
KE and plateaus ~0.5 m/s short of (I) with a residual splitting entropy that does not vanish; and an
explicit predictor-corrector on the target is unstable as `relax → 1` (this is where the spurious
`Da·ds`-instability lived — the shipped *implicit* scheme does not have it).
